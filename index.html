<about_me>

robotics and AI researcher

I'm Elias — an engineer and researcher from Athens. I recently graduated from the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at NTUA, where I spent six years building things, breaking them, and occasionally understanding why.

I care about making robots that work in the real world. Most of my time goes into reading papers, running experiments, and staring at loss curves. Outside of that, I enjoy long walks, good coffee, and collecting wallpapers I never actually use as wallpapers.

This site is mostly for keeping track of what I've done and where I'm going. If something here interests you, feel free to reach out.

My research focuses on making learning-based robotic systems work reliably outside controlled settings. Right now I'm most interested in how to give VLA models a better grasp of physical reality — things like object dynamics, reachability constraints, and whether a proposed action is actually feasible. Related to this is the question of continual learning: how do you let a policy accumulate knowledge across deployments without overwriting what it already knows? I'm also drawn to the distribution shift problem under behavioural cloning, where compounding errors push robots into states they've never seen, and to the broader question of what data and architecture choices actually drive generalisation in robotic pre-training.
